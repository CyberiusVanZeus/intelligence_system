The Future of the Fertilizer Sector in Tunis

At a regional workshop held in Tunis from June 24th to 26th, 2025, organized by the Arab Fertilizer Union in partnership with the Tunisian chemical complex, experts from across the Arab world gathered to address the significant changes occurring in the sector. As global crises accelerate, particularly in food, water, energy, and the environment, these experts are tackling vital survival challenges on a global scale. The workshop aims to develop a strategic roadmap based on six major pillars to navigate these challenges.

The Arab Fertilizer Union's ambitious roadmap focuses on six major areas, with food security topping the list. Arab countries play a crucial role, contributing over 33% of the global fertilizer supply to combat hunger affecting nearly a billion people worldwide. The long-term goal is to establish a sustainable agricultural model by 2050, reducing hunger while ensuring more intelligent resource use. Additionally, the workshop addresses urgent issues such as water scarcity, energy management amidst rising prices and ecological transitions, climate concerns, and the integration of sustainability across all levels of the fertilizer industry.
